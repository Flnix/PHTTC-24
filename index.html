Rotary Club of Madras Mid-town (RCMM), chartered in 1972, is one among the

34,000 clubs of Rotary International. RCMM, ranked as one of the premier clubs

in Rotary District 3233, has 50+ members currently, consisting of professionals

and businessman from across industries. The club continues to execute

many long sustaining service projects, some with longevity of 10 to 15 years,

significantly impacting the lives of thousands of beneficiaries in and around

Chennai. RCMM celebrated its golden jubilee (50th) year in the Rotary Year

2021- 22.

Over 4 decades, RCMM continues to serve the community through: Health

Care, Literacy &Education, Water &Sanitation, Integrated Rural Development,

Environmental programs, Youth Development and many more initiatives.

Rotary Club of Madras Midtown’s Long-Term initiatives are Cancer Care for the

last 27 years (www.rotarycancercare.org), Women’s Vocational Training Centre

at Choolai, Stop TB Awareness Program, Village Development (Medical Centre

&KG school), Digital Literacy Program, Go-Green - planting of trees. Rotary Club

of Madras Mid-Town looks forward to your participation that will give HOPE to

many and bring SMILES on the faces of the beneficiaries.

Paul P Harris, the father of the Rotary Movement, was born on April 19, 1868.

When he passed away on January 27, 1947, he left behind a rich legacy of fellowship and brotherhood for international understanding and service to humanity. The seed he sowed in 1905 has blossomed into a large tree covering 200 geographical regions, with over 32,000 clubs and a membership of over 1.2 million.

Given here are a few excerpts from his speeches and writings that are as relevant in today's world as they were during his lifetime:

  1. "This is a changing world; we must be prepared to change with it. The story of Rotary will have to be written again and again."

  2. On "Followship": "Followship is wonderful; it illuminates life's pathway, spreads good cheer, and is worth a high price."

  3. On "Friendship": "Friendship was the foundation rock on which Rotary was built, and tolerance is the element that holds it together."

  4. On "Business": "In the Rotary plan, business is an important part of life, but it is not the all of life. He whose vision extends no further than his field of business is to be pitied."
